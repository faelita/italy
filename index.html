Lewis Model

According to Lewis' model, both Italy and Colombia are part of the Multi - active, Multi - active people are people - oriented, lots of body language, goes for all-embracing solutions, speech for opinions and more. Despite being part of the same category, I would say that if an Italian comes to study in Barranquilla could have certain difficulties, some of these are: Firstly, the language, even if the person knows Spanish, the people from the coast usually speak very fast, skip some parts of the words and have words that are only heard here, making it difficult to understand that person coming from Italy. Secondly, if it is a person who drives a car, in Italy the driver is on the right, while here the driver is on the left, so they could have problems when driving, it could even cause an accident. Finally, I would say that they would have difficulties getting used to the food, especially if they go to a restaurant to eat pasta or pizza.

Non-verbal communication

It had never occurred to me that non-verbal communication could vary depending on the country, as a first example, in Italy they have a popularly known gesture, "la pigna" is the most typical Italian cliché. It is used to accompany a question or to indicate a doubt. In Colombia this gesture is not seen in many people, in fact, if someone uses it is because he is imitating an Italian. As a second example, in Italy if you want to say that something is very tasty (in this case talking about food), point your index finger against your cheek and rotate it on itself several times. In Colombia this is not seen, it is the first time I hear it. And as a last example, in Italy to express that you can't stand someone place your hand in front of your chest in a vertical position with your fingers extended downwards. Give a couple or three blows on the sternum making an exasperated face. With these examples we can see that non-verbal communication can vary depending on the country we are in, being that both a pro and a con for communication as it can favor us or make us pass a misunderstanding.
